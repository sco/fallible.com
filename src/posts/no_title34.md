"I really like the way you wrote 'where the house joins the world,'" Doug says. "Yes, that's the phrase I like, too," I say. "Too bad I have to write 350 words to get one good phrase..." On second thought, it would be even worse to skip the trouble, the frustration, the humiliation of writing the 350 ho-hum words, and end up with no good phrases, ever. So, I keep on writing.
